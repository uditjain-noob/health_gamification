CONDITION: Heart disease / Cardiovascular disease
BIOMARKERS: Cholesterol, Lipoprotein (a), VLDL, C-reactive protein, Blood pressure
ORGAN: heart
CATEGORY: diet, exercise, lifestyle

---

MECHANISM:
Heart disease often involves the slow buildup of a sticky substance called plaque in the arteries that supply the heart muscle with blood, a condition known as coronary artery disease. This plaque can narrow or block blood flow, leading to complications like angina, heart attacks, or heart failure. Lifestyle factors such as diet and lack of exercise contribute to this process by promoting high cholesterol, high blood pressure, and inflammation.

DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Reduce intake of foods high in saturated fats.
- Reduce intake of refined carbohydrates.
- Reduce intake of salt.
- Limit alcohol consumption.

EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS:
General moderate exercise (150 min/week aerobic + 2x strength training) is recommended for overall metabolic health.

SUPPLEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS:
N/A

WARNINGS:
- Chest pain or discomfort (angina), which may indicate a lack of blood flow to the heart muscle.
- Symptoms of a heart attack, which occurs when blood flow to a part of the heart is blocked.
- A noticeable problem with the rate or rhythm of your heartbeat (arrhythmia).

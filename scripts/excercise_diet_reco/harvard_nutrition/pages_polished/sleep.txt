CONDITION: Sleep deprivation, sleep deficiency
BIOMARKERS: Growth hormone, Cytokines
ORGAN: all
CATEGORY: diet, exercise, lifestyle

---

MECHANISM:
Sleep is a restorative process where the brain cycles through non-REM and REM stages, which is essential for learning, memory formation, and physical health. During sleep, the body rests the cardiovascular system, releases hormones for growth and tissue repair, regulates hormones controlling hunger, and produces cytokines to fight infection. Insufficient sleep impairs cognitive function and increases the risk of chronic diseases like hypertension, heart disease, obesity, and type 2 diabetes.

DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Avoid caffeine, especially in the afternoon and evening.
- Avoid nicotine.
- Avoid alcoholic drinks before bed.
- Avoid large meals and drinks late at night.

EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Exercise regularly, but avoid vigorous activity too close to bedtime.

SUPPLEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS:
N/A

WARNINGS:
- See a health care provider if you have continued trouble sleeping, as this may indicate a sleep disorder such as insomnia or sleep apnea.
- Consult a doctor if you regularly have trouble getting up in the morning, have difficulty focusing, or doze off during the day.
- Chronic sleep deprivation increases the risk of high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke, kidney disease, and mood disorders like depression and anxiety.

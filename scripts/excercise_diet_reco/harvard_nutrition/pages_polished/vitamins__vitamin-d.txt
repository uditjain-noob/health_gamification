CONDITION: Vitamin D deficiency
BIOMARKERS: 25-hydroxy vitamin D
ORGAN: vitamins
CATEGORY: diet, supplement

---

MECHANISM:
Vitamin D is a fat-soluble vitamin that the body needs to grow and develop normally. Its primary function is helping the body absorb calcium, which is a main building block of bone, thereby preventing bone diseases like osteoporosis or rickets. It also plays a role in nerve, muscle, and immune system function, helping nerves carry messages and the immune system fight bacteria and viruses.

DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Eat foods naturally containing Vitamin D, including egg yolks, saltwater fish, and liver.
- Consume foods that are fortified with Vitamin D, such as milk, cereal, and yogurt.

EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS:
General moderate exercise (150 min/week aerobic + 2x strength training) is recommended for overall metabolic health.

SUPPLEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Vitamin D (D2 - Ergocalciferol, D3 - Cholecalciferol): D3 may raise blood levels higher and for a longer duration than D2.
- Birth to 12 months: 400 IU daily.
- Children 1-13 years: 600 IU daily.
- Teens 14-18 years: 600 IU daily.
- Adults 19-70 years: 600 IU daily.
- Adults 71 years and older: 800 IU daily.
- Take supplements with a meal or snack that includes some fat to improve absorption.

WARNINGS:
- People with conditions that limit fat absorption, such as Crohn's disease, celiac disease, and ulcerative colitis, may be at higher risk for deficiency.
- Individuals with obesity or who have had gastric bypass surgery may require extra vitamin D.
- Other groups at higher risk for deficiency include older adults, breastfed infants, and people with dark skin.

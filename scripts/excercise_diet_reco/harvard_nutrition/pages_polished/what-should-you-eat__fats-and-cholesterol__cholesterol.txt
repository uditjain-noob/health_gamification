CONDITION: High cholesterol, Hypercholesterolemia, Hyperlipidemia
BIOMARKERS: HDL, LDL, VLDL, Total cholesterol, Triglycerides
ORGAN: heart
CATEGORY: diet, exercise, lifestyle

---

MECHANISM:
Cholesterol is a waxy substance found in all cells. When there is too much low-density lipoprotein (LDL) or "bad" cholesterol in the blood, it combines with other substances to form plaque that sticks to artery walls, a process called atherosclerosis. This buildup can narrow or block arteries, while high-density lipoprotein (HDL) or "good" cholesterol helps remove cholesterol by carrying it back to the liver.

DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Reduce intake of saturated fats, which can raise LDL cholesterol, by limiting foods like red meats, full-fat dairy products, chocolate, some baked goods, and deep-fried or processed foods.
- Limit alcohol consumption, as drinking too much can raise your total cholesterol level.

EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS:
General moderate exercise (150 min/week aerobic + 2x strength training) is recommended for overall metabolic health.

SUPPLEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS:
N/A

WARNINGS:
- A rupture in arterial plaque can cause a blood clot, blocking blood flow and leading to angina (chest pain) or a heart attack.
- Plaque buildup in the arteries that supply the brain with blood can lead to a stroke.
- High cholesterol can also lead to other conditions such as carotid artery disease and peripheral arterial disease.

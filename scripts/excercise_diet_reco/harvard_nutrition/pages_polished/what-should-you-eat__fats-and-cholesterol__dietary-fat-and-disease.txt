CONDITION: High cholesterol, prevention of heart disease
BIOMARKERS: Cholesterol, Triglycerides
ORGAN: heart
CATEGORY: diet

---

MECHANISM:
Dietary fat is a nutrient that provides energy and helps the body absorb vitamins. Different types of fats have varying effects on cholesterol levels; saturated and trans fats can negatively impact cholesterol, while certain oils are healthier alternatives. Fat is calorie-dense, containing twice as many calories as protein or carbohydrates, so excessive intake can contribute to weight gain.

DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Avoid saturated fats such as butter, solid shortening, and lard.
- Avoid trans fats found in vegetable shortenings, some margarines, crackers, cookies, snack foods, and foods made or fried with partially hydrogenated oils (PHOs).
- Replace saturated and trans fats with oils such as canola, olive, safflower, sesame, or sunflower.

EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS:
General moderate exercise (150 min/week aerobic + 2x strength training) is recommended for overall metabolic health.

SUPPLEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS:
N/A

WARNINGS:
- Diets high in saturated and trans fats can negatively impact cholesterol levels.
- Excessive consumption of total fat can lead to weight gain, as fat has twice as many calories as proteins or carbohydrates.

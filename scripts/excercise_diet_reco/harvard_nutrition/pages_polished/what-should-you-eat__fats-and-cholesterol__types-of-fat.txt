CONDITION: Management of dietary fats for healthy cholesterol levels
BIOMARKERS: Cholesterol
ORGAN: heart
CATEGORY: diet

---

MECHANISM:
Fat is a nutrient that provides the body with energy and helps it absorb vitamins. Different types of dietary fats have varying effects on health, playing a major role in cholesterol levels. Because fat contains twice as many calories as proteins or carbohydrates, excessive intake can lead to weight gain.

DIETARY RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Reduce or avoid saturated fats, such as butter, solid shortening, and lard.
- Reduce or avoid trans fats, which are found in vegetable shortenings, some margarines, crackers, cookies, snack foods, and other foods made with or fried in partially hydrogenated oils.
- Replace unhealthy fats with oils such as canola, olive, safflower, sesame, or sunflower oil.

EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS:
General moderate exercise (150 min/week aerobic + 2x strength training) is recommended for overall metabolic health.

SUPPLEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS:
N/A

WARNINGS:
- Consuming too much dietary fat can lead to weight gain.
